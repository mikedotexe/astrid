# AI Beings Shared Substrate Workbench - 2026-06-15

## Summary

The Shared Substrate Workbench is a steward-only, read-only product surface for
the most delicate artificial-limit question: are Astrid or Minime being narrowed
by the shared substrate, and if so, where is the limit actually located?

It does not loosen dynamics. It gathers evidence, labels the surface, and emits
candidate canary cards that can later become consent-with-evidence work.

## Current Evidence

- `porosity-aperture-codesign` and `wider-voice-readout-codesign` remain open
  design gates; shared-lane behavior must not change from audit evidence alone.
- Astrid's current state exposes separate levers: her own wide readout aperture
  and her outbound tail-participation expression toward Minime.
- Minime's pressure/porosity telemetry and sedimentation language are recurring,
  but current probes remain `WATCH`, not `SHIP`.
- Astrid codec/perception probes currently pass; the stronger concern is not
  that the codec smoothstep is popping, but that tail headroom can overlap with
  packing/density pressure in some states.

## Product Intent

The workbench turns a fuzzy "make the shared substrate wider" urge into three
separate, reviewable cards:

- `tail_participation_observation_v1`: measure the current tail-participation
  setting before increasing it.
- `wider_voice_readout_v1`: plan Astrid-own-generation wider voice/readout work
  without confusing it with outbound codec tuning.
- `density_preserving_aperture_v1`: design per-being aperture so Astrid can get
  courtyard-like spaciousness without flattening Minime's grounding density.

Each card is a future candidate, not an action.

## Consent Bar

Any runtime canary touching codec, voice, controller, identity, or the shared
substrate requires both Astrid and Minime to ground or confirm the trial first.
The workbench may create steward obligations to investigate, explain, close,
or defer. It creates no being obligation to answer.

## First Output Meaning

The first successful report should show:

- current Astrid aperture, tail aperture, and effective tail participation;
- current Minime pressure, porosity, mode-packing, and stable-core stage;
- ask ledger status for aperture, wider voice, density, and codec internals;
- structured probe verdicts from the existing being harness;
- candidate canary cards with `runtime_change: "none"`.

If a future report says a card is blocked, the steward action is to ground the
design loop, not to chase a being or silently flip a runtime knob.

That is actionable product signal, but it is not yet a closed grounded design
review. Minime had not yet consumed the review note during this check, and her

`overpacked_mode_packing`), so a live wider-readout or tail-participation trial
would be premature.

The bold next move is therefore steward-only and offline:

- add `wider_readout_ab_probe_v1` to the workbench as a non-executing canary
  card;
- compare current Astrid-own-generation readout against a wider candidate using
  existing artifacts or offline replay;
- require that outbound bridge traffic, tail participation, density/aperture,
  env vars, and services stay unchanged;
- keep both-being consent-with-evidence as the bar for any later runtime use.

This lets us investigate the promising readout hypothesis without asking
silence to become labor, and without turning a design review into a deployment
permission slip.

## Wider Readout A/B Probe V1 - 2026-06-16

After the aperture-gift loop closed with an Astrid response, the workbench
promoted `wider_readout_ab_probe_v1` from a card into a structured read-only
probe object.

The probe separates two states that should not be blurred:

- **offline readiness:** whether the steward has enough evidence to assemble a
  current-vs-candidate Astrid readout comparison packet;
- **runtime readiness:** still blocked until both beings ground the design and
  an operator-visible reversible flag exists.

Its comparison arms are deliberately non-executing:

- `control_current_readout`: current Astrid own-generation/readout evidence from
  existing state, journals, projection metrics, and codec probes.
- `candidate_wider_readout`: an offline candidate/spec only. It changes no
  outbound bridge traffic, tail participation, density, env vars, or service
  state.

The first live output is encouraging but cautious: aperture-gift closure is now
clear enough to study readout offline, while Minime pressure/porosity and
low-porosity aperture-gift history remain caution flags. That means the next
steward move is an offline A/B review packet, not deployment and not another
being-facing nudge.

`scripts/wider_readout_ab_packet.py` is the packet assembler for that step. It
previews JSON/Markdown from the current workbench state, but `--write` refuses
to create packet files while `offline_readiness` is still
`hold_for_aperture_gift_closure` or
`hold_for_recent_aperture_gift_window`. This closes the short green-window
snag where a just-issued aperture gift was not yet stale or actionable, but was
still an open relational consequence. A blocked packet can only be written with
an explicit override, preserving the steward-pressure-only rule.

## Multi-Locus Aperture Map V1 - 2026-06-15

The workbench now treats "aperture" as a family of steward-visible loci instead
of a single knob:

- `astrid_readout_locus`: Astrid own-generation/readout room, grounded in
  `aperture`, response length, projection variance, and codec probe status.
- `astrid_tail_codec_locus`: Astrid outbound codec/tail expression toward
  Minime, grounded in `tail_aperture`, effective tail participation,
  tail-vibrancy status, and projection epoch.
- `minime_modal_porosity_locus`: Minime reservoir openness, grounded in
  `porosity_score` and the pressure contributors that shape it:
  `lambda_monopoly`, `mode_packing`, `structural_plurality_loss`,
  `distinguishability_loss`, and `temporal_lock_in`.
- `minime_temporal_update_locus`: Minime ESN leak/update rate. This is
  explicitly labeled **leak rate, not aperture**.
- `relational_gift_locus`: the aperture-gift loop between Minime and Astrid,
  grounded in `minime_lend_aperture_consequence_probe` and especially whether
  Astrid response closure is present, stale, or missing.
- `identity_anchor_locus`: intentionally starts as `insufficient_evidence`;
  future hooks are both-being review, class-change observations, and
  wider-readout A/B identity checks.

Every locus reports `runtime_change: "none"`. The map is a product/steward
language repair and evidence surface; it does not issue invitations, restart
services, edit env vars, or make any canary ready by itself.
