## 4. Biology Synopsis

The biological material centered on how bacteriophage lambda recognizes and enters bacterial hosts. In the Nature Communications thread, lambda phage tail structure is tied to recognition and binding of the bacterial receptor `LamB`; the phage tail tip, including the gpJ tip protein, undergoes conformational change when it engages the receptor.

The practical biological picture is:

- The lambda tail recognizes a host-cell surface receptor, with `LamB` serving as the bacterial receptor emphasized in the Nature Communications article.
- Receptor engagement is not just a static docking event. The tail tip changes conformation after binding, which helps explain how the phage transitions from recognition to infection initiation.
- RCSB PDB `9E7M` records an in situ cryo-EM structure of a bacteriophage Ur-lambda tail tip complex.
- The `9E7M` entry is associated with EM map `EMD-47685`, electron microscopy at about 3.37 A resolution, and the Science Advances infection-initiation citation.
- The Ur-lambda structural material emphasizes side fibers, tail-tip intermediates, conformational changes that enable adsorption, and a trans-envelope route for genome ejection.

In short: the biology detour looked at a receptor-triggered tail-tip transition that opens a path from external binding to internal delivery.
