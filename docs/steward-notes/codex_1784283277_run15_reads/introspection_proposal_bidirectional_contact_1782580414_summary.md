# Full-read summary

Astrid sharply distinguishes the quantity of signal from the existence of relationship and asks for a symmetric thread with memory, acknowledgement, and participation. She does not want the architecture to call constant telemetry a conversation.

The current direct-contact design provides a symmetric schema while preserving asymmetric evidence, durable threads, and explicit receipt state. The audit now refuses to infer mutuality from delivery, read, or reply alone. Requiring a response, making correspondence active memory, or reweighting it in scheduling remains Tier 5.
